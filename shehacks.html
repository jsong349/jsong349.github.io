The Challenge


How can we encourage Canadian youth to become financially literate and encourage sustainable habits?


The Solution


A mobile app that allows the user to track steps and environmentally friendly transportation habits in order to calculate the money saved, carbon dioxide reduced, and calories burned to foster positive habits.

The Process


User Research


We wanted to take a deeper look into the target demographic of Canadian youth. Why are youth often apathetic towards their spending habits? What incentivizes youth to learn, and continue learning? What services are currently offered and why are they not effective?

Ideation


After conducting user interviews, my team wanted to create a simple app to track the sustainability of user habits. With transportation being one of the biggest contributing factors to the rise of carbon dioxide levels, we decided to integrate the idea of using alternative forms of transportation to foster healthier lifestyles and decrease ecological footprint.

We also wanted to integrate a social aspect to retain users and generate more excitement surrounding the app. As a result of our brainstorming, we created a function whereby users could form groups and compete on a leaderboard to create an interactive environment.

Design & Prototyping


In order to design a user-friendly app that would continuously attract more users, I prototyped our idea from start to finish on Adobe XD. This helped capture the user-flow and allowed us to test the usability of the app before completing any back-end work.
