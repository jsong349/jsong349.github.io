QuickBill

Scotiabank Hacks 2020 | 1st Place Overall Hack

QuickBill extends the payment services of a branch to the palm of your hand through the Scotia app. By integrating the functionality of machine learning driven optical character recognition, we automatically identify the payee, account number, and amount due. You can simply take a photo of your bill within the app, or forward your bill via email for processing - QuickBill handles the rest!

The Challenge

The Solution


A new feature on the Scotiabank banking app that allows the user to pay bills with a few swipes. By setting up email forwarding or taking a quick picture of your bill, all bill payments are now centralized on the banking app.

The Process


User Research


We wanted to take a deeper look into our target demographics of seniors, newcomers, and people on-the-go. Why are bill payments so frustrating? What services are currently offered and why are they not effective? Why are people constantly late on payments?


Through interviewing parents and grandparents, we discovered a few main takeaways:


  • The bill payments process is a hassle ‚Äì from receiving multiple bills in your mailbox or email, to typing out the numbers online, to finally paying a bill, then repeating that process again for every payment, the process is time consuming

  • People are looking for more digital banking solutions ‚Äì the times of waiting in a long line at a banking branch are over, people are looking for quicker solutions from the comfort of their own home

  • Dealing with payments is messy ‚Äì users felt it was difficult to keep track of payments and their deadlines

Ideation


With an increasing number of Scotia customers utilizing digital banking, my team wanted to create a new feature to tackle the issue of bill payments with a simple app feature. We understood that payments are typically received digitally, through monthly emails or physically, to the user's mailbox. We then set out to create a solution that addressed both sides of bill payment.



As a result of our brainstorming, we decided to create a direct forwarding feature to address the payments received digitally and an image recognition feature to address the payments received physically.

Design & Prototyping


In order to design a user-friendly feature that would attract more users and increase user engagement in our existing users, I prototyped our idea from start to finish on Figma. This allowed us to evaluate technical feasibility and user specification.


Let‚Äôs take a look through some of the key features we deployed:


DIRECT FORWARDING

Presenting all bills on a centralized page


With this new feature, users can set up forwarding from their personal email inbox directly to their Scotia app. Worried about privacy? QuickBill allows users to approve senders before bill payments can proceed. It's that simple! Now all bill payments will be displayed right on the Scotia app.


Users can then pay bills individually, or all at once, mitigating the issue of hassle and disorganization of traditional bill payment.


IMAGE RECOGNITION

Allowing in-app automatic payments


Instead of adding a new payee, users can click onto the "Capture bills by image recognition" feature, and take a photo of their paper bill right on the app. The information will then automatically populate, and the user can pay on the spot!


Rather than wasting time typing out every bill separately, this new feature now allows user to save time with greater accuracy.
